- (decision) Product: tv-time-2, a TV/streaming tracker. Working mode: coaching path.
- (decision) Form-factor: mobile app (phone-first).
- (decision) Stakes: consumer product — targeting real strangers; must feel trustworthy and complete.
- (decision) The itch / motivation: the original TV Time was discontinued; tv-time-2 is a spiritual successor to fill that hole.
- (decision) Scope: tracks BOTH films and TV shows (not TV-only).
- (decision) Core loop / the one move: mark a film or show as watched, rate it, and maybe see what to watch next.
- (decision) Vibe/direction: cozy / chill / vintage. User open to exploring the vintage direction.
- (decision) Social scope: LIGHT social — optional friends + a simple activity feed of what people watched. Not a full network.
- (decision) Discovery ('what to watch next'): BOTH — a self-curated watchlist AND a recommendations shelf based on ratings/history.
- (decision) Rating: TV Time-style hybrid — a 5-star rating (overall quality) PLUS mood-emoji reactions (how it made you feel, e.g. cried/shook/laughed). Emojis add feed personality.
- (assumption) [ASSUMPTION] Mood emojis are a small curated set (not free emoji picker); ratings apply to films & whole titles, and reactions may also apply per-episode as TV Time did. To confirm.
- (decision) IA / bottom nav (mobile, 5 slots): Home · Diary · (+) fast-add center · Feed · Profile. Center (+) = one-tap 'I just watched something' → search → log → 5 stars → mood emoji.
- (decision) Home surface hosts: shows-in-progress / Up Next, the Watchlist shelf, and a recommendations shelf. Watchlist lives on Home (always in sight when deciding what to watch).
- (decision) Confirmed surfaces: Home/Up Next, Search+Add, Title detail (film or show w/ seasons+episodes), Diary/history, Feed (friends activity), Profile (stats, favorites, watchlist, friends).
- (decision) Visual direction signal: user drawn to 'Faded Technicolor' (light: dusty teal/coral/mustard) AND 'VHS Dusk' (dark: plum/magenta/cyan neon) — the saturated, playful, poster-y end of vintage. Interpreting as ONE system with light+dark modes (Technicolor by day, VHS by night).
- (decision) DARK MODE LOCKED: 'VHS Dusk' palette approved as-is — bg #16131e, surface #211c2c, primary magenta #ec5a92, cool cyan #45c2cf, gold star #f2c14e, text #ece7f2, muted #8a82a0. This is the hero mode.
- (change) Light mode: background #efe9df rejected as too brown/cream. Keep Technicolor accents (coral/teal/gold) but move to a cleaner, cooler/lighter canvas. Rendering options.
- (decision) LIGHT MODE LOCKED: 'Paper White' — bg #FAF9F7, surface #FFFFFF, primary coral #E0654F, cool teal #2F8F88, gold star #DCA82E, ink #262B2A, muted #7E857F. Cozy without brown; accents pop.
- (decision) COLOR SYSTEM COMPLETE ('Retro Technicolor'): light=Paper White/Technicolor, dark=VHS Dusk. Shared gold star + poster gradients carry the identity across both modes. Dark is the hero.
- (assumption) [ASSUMPTION] Accent role mapping is consistent across modes: primary/CTA = coral(light)/magenta(dark); cool secondary = teal(light)/cyan(dark); rating stars = gold in both. Corner radius ~13-14px, soft. To confirm during component pass.
- (decision) TYPOGRAPHY LOCKED: Display = Fraunces (logo, big titles, section headings — soft characterful vintage serif, use optical sizing + heavier weights ~700-800). Body/UI = DM Sans (meta, buttons, labels, long text — clean, legible). 'Cozy characterful / modern-vintage' register.
- (event) Visual identity core settled: Retro Technicolor color system (light+dark) + Fraunces/DM Sans type. Ready to pivot to experience/behavior + key flows.
- (decision) Key concerns confirmed: (1) NEW-EPISODE NOTIFICATIONS — push when a tracked show drops a new episode (retention driver); (2) FAST BULK LOGGING — mark many episodes/a whole season watched at once. NOT prioritized: offline-first, and accessibility beyond the standard consumer floor (still design to WCAG AA basics).
- (decision) Data source: external TMDB-style catalog API — titles, posters, episode lists, metadata pulled from it. Drives search, title detail, episode tracking.
- (assumption) [ASSUMPTION] Notifications are opt-in per-show or global toggle; a 'notify me about new episodes' control lives on the title-detail screen and/or a settings surface. To confirm.
- (decision) KEY FLOW 1 'The bedtime log' (core loop) APPROVED. Protagonist Léa, 27. Home>Up Next surfaces current show>tap card>next episode pre-selected>one-tap Watched>optional rate(5 gold stars)+mood emoji>climax: gentle progress/reward beat ('X episodes this year' + 'add next to Up Next?')>optional save a rec to watchlist. Target: <15s, faster-than-forgetting.
- (decision) Rating friction: OPTIONAL. 'Watched' commits instantly; rating+mood prompt is offered but one-tap dismissable, never blocks. Principle: cozy, not nagging.
- (decision) Mood emoji set (draft): 😭 cried, 😱 shook, 🤣 laughed, 🥹 touched, 😌 satisfied — small curated set, not a full picker. To finalize during component pass.
- (decision) KEY FLOWS 2-4 APPROVED. J2 'Binge catch-up' (Théo,24): show>season>'Mark whole season watched'>pre-checked sheet>one tap>optional season rating; bingeing rewarded. J3 'Adding new' (Léa): center(+)>search(TMDB)>title detail>'Add to Watchlist' OR 'I'm watching this'. J4 'Friends feed' (Théo): Feed tab>friend's rating+mood>tap>❤️ save to own watchlist; word-of-mouth discovery, not algorithm.
- (decision) NOTIFICATIONS trigger: (1) explicit bell toggle on title-detail (user control, independent of tracking) + (2) smart contextual dialog when user finishes the last available episode/season ('You're all caught up on X — notify you when new drops?'). Best-of-both: control + well-timed nudge.
- (override) Notify trigger is NOT auto-on-tracking (rejected). Chosen: explicit bell + caught-up dialog.
- (decision) VOICE & TONE: Warm & personal — cozy, encouraging, lightly sentimental ('Your story starts here. What did you watch tonight?'). Emoji allowed but gentle. Not cheeky-witty, not coldly minimal.
